To use CoMaps with Android Auto, you need at least an Android version 9.0 (Pie) or later. Also, Google requires that CoMaps be downloaded from the Google Play Store, or at least appear to be so.

There are some reported workarounds to install an app "from" Google Play without actually using Google Play, but this is beyond the scope of what CoMaps can recommend or support, and the situation seems to change more often than we can keep track. 

This is a special Android Auto restriction on navigation and other apps that are allowed to display while driving; we have to adhere to extra review guidelines for drivers' safety and usability, like laying out the UI nicely, handling vehicle console button-presses as expected, etc.

Please note that while some other types of applications for Android Auto are less strictly regulated (and thus work in Android Auto when installed from F-Droid or other sources), these stronger restrictions apply to all navigation apps.
